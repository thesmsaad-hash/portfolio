One of the most common things I hear from new clients is: "I just need a video editor." But what they actually need is both a video editor *and* a motion graphics artist — and they're very different skill sets.

Let me break it down.

## Video Editing: The Architecture

Video editing is the structure. It's the art of:
- Selecting the best takes
- Pacing the story through cuts
- Color grading for mood
- Mixing audio
- Managing transitions

Think of the video editor as the architect of your content. They decide what goes where and why.

**Primary tools:** Premiere Pro, DaVinci Resolve, Final Cut Pro

## Motion Graphics: The Decoration

Motion graphics is the visual language layered on top. It includes:
- Animated titles and lower thirds
- Logo animations
- Kinetic typography
- Infographics that move
- Transitions that brand

A motion graphics artist takes still design elements and gives them life through animation, timing, and physics.

**Primary tools:** After Effects, Motion, Blender

## Why You Need Both

Here's the truth: a video without motion graphics looks like raw footage. Motion graphics without video editing looks like a slide deck.

The magic happens at the intersection. When you have:
- A well-paced edit WITH branded lower thirds
- Strong color grading WITH animated call-to-actions
- Punchy cuts WITH kinetic text that emphasizes key points

That's when content goes from good to unforgettable.

## The 80/20 Rule I Apply

For most YouTube and social content, I apply this split:
- **80% video editing** — the core story, pacing, audio
- **20% motion graphics** — accents, branding, emphasis

For brand videos and commercials, it flips:
- **40% video editing** — the foundation
- **60% motion graphics** — the spectacle

## How to Know What You Need

**You primarily need video editing if:**
- You're creating talking-head or vlog content
- Your video is interview or podcast based
- You have lots of footage to organize

**You primarily need motion graphics if:**
- You're creating explainer videos
- You need animated logos or intros
- Your content requires visualizing concepts

**You need both if:**
- You're building a YouTube channel brand
- You're creating commercial or brand content
- You want content that stands out from competitors
